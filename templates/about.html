About this app and how to use

Please take a minute to read this and better understand how to use this workout app!
If you have questions or need help, email me at cameronjmacLean@gmail.com

Tabata is a 4 minute exercise routine. You exercise for 20 seconds, rest for 10 and you do this cycle 8 times.

1 Active for 20 Rest for 10
2 Active for 20 Rest for 10 1 minute
3 Active for 20 Rest for 10
4 Active for 20 Rest for 10 2 minutes
5 Active for 20 Rest for 10
6 Active for 20 Rest for 10 3 minutes
7 Active for 20 Rest for 10
8 Active for 20 Rest for 10 4 minutes

If this is your first time doing an exercise, awesome! You're in uncharted waters so take the first time doing an exercise to get a solid foundation to work off of and perfect your movement.

Start at a reasonable number of reps, a low weight and do not worry about having high or impressive numbers. That doesn't matter.

focus on form and how the whole routine feels

What matters most is having a good foundation that you can build on next time. You won't have that foundation if you have to bail before completing the Tabata routine.

How To Record A Workout

With your exercise chosen, predetermine the number of reps you will want to complete in each cycle and your goal is to do those amount of reps 8 times.

If you start to feel weak on or before set 4 during your reps, drop the total number of reps down by one. For example:

  1. You set out to do 5 dips on workout rings.
  2. You feel weak after completing the 3rd set.
  3. On your 4th set and going forward, do 4 dips instead of 5.
  4. You'll record for the routine 3 sets at 5 reps.
In the example above, if you feel on set 7 that you can go back up to 5 to complete the routine, doing 5 reps on both set 7 and 8, great! Do it! And you would record that routine as 5 sets at 5 reps.

Best Practices

  • High Method: Get the heart rate high to see muscle growth and a strong heart for cardio strength
  • Low Methdo: Do slow movement and 4-5 reps to focus on improving muscular strength
  • Vary the high and low methods - don't just focus on a single method
  • In general, Compound movement > Isolated movement
  • In general, Posterior focus > Anterior focus
  • No matter what, never compromise your form

Lastly, this is just one of many effective and healthy ways to maintain being fit and active. I found this program to work for me and it may work for you too! Or it may not. Give it a try but I am not suggesting this is the *only* way.
